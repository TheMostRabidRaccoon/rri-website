BNP — 2-Year Longitudinal Trend

Chronically >400 for 2+ years with swings correlating to volume status. Today’s 1,520 is near historical peak (1,567 in 01/2024). Confirms active decompensation—not a new diagnosis.

Aug 2023: 1,061 → Jan 2024: 1,567 (peak) → Sep 2024: 1,347 → Dec 2025: 773 → Today: 1,520

HS Troponin I — Chronic Elevation, NOT ACS

Chronic range 25–67 ng/L over 2+ years. Today’s 67→66 is flat on serial draw. Type 2 MI physiology from cardiorenal syndrome—not acute plaque rupture. A provider seeing only today’s troponin of 67 without this trend might pursue an unnecessary ACS workup.

Aug 2023: 47 → Sep 2023: 25 → Jan 2024: 50 → Sep 2024: 58 (peak) → Today: 67→66 (flat serial)

Echocardiography — Grade 3 Diastolic Dysfunction

Grade 3 (restrictive) pattern on two consecutive echos. This is why the patient cannot tolerate aggressive ultrafiltration during dialysis. The ED note documents “admit for dialysis”—but without this context, the admitting team won’t know to modify the HD prescription.

EF: 45-50% (pre-CABG) → 50% (post-CABG) → 50-55% (12/2023) · AV velocity trending: 1.2 → 2.0 → 1.8 m/s

Renal Trajectory — GFR Loss 4x Normal Rate

GFR decline: 51 → 7 mL/min over ~3 years = loss of ~15 mL/min/year. Normal age-related decline is 2–4 mL/min/year. Kidneys sustained acute injury during CABG (hypotension on bypass) and never recovered, superimposed on 14 years of completely unmanaged diabetes.

Cr: 1.4 (2022) → 1.5 (pre-CABG) → mid-2s (post-op) → 4.8-5.7 (2024) → 6.56 (12/2025) → 8.60 (today)

Dialysis Tolerance — Chronic UF Shortfall

Consistently falls short of UF goals due to restrictive physiology. UF goals 2,400–4,500 mL, achieved 1,837–3,906 mL. Cannot tolerate aggressive fluid removal. This admission: UF goal reduced from 2,100 to 1,500 mL due to cramping, followed by post-HD hypoglycemic crash (glucose 46–51, required D50W rescue).

Multi-Specialty Non-Adherence — Documented Pattern

Formal diagnosis on problem list. Documented independently by nephrology (14 years no DM care, shortens HD), cardiology (stopped all GDMT x 2 months), and podiatry (incomplete vascular workup). Self-dosed doubled Lasix today. This changes disposition planning from “schedule follow-up” to “verify dialysis chair before discharge.”

Anansi Clinical Synthesis

Acute decompensated heart failure secondary to volume overload from missed hemodialysis × 6 days. BNP 1,520 (near historical peak). Chronic combined systolic and diastolic CHF with Grade 3 restrictive diastolic dysfunction. The patient’s inability to tolerate interdialytic intervals is not a dialysis problem—it is a cardiac problem manifesting through dialysis.

Hypertensive urgency with SBP escalating 172→206 over 2 hours. Troponins stable serial (67→66)—chronic baseline, NOT ACS. Glucose 478, non-DKA. Active limb-threatening vascular disease with incomplete workup. Documented multi-specialty non-adherence impacting every downstream decision.

12 active problems identified. 16 ICD-10 codes captured. E/M: 99215 (Level 5). Recommend: emergent HD with modified UF protocol, confirm outpatient HD chair before discharge, urgent cardiology re-establishment for interval echo and graft patency evaluation, complete vascular workup for limb threat, optimize insulin regimen, cannabis counseling.
